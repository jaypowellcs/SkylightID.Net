Identifying what type of skylights you have

This section will help decide on what type of skylight you have.

Is your skylight glass or arylic?

Arylic Skylights

Arylic skylights will consist of a plastic top that can either be a bronze/clear arylic or white/clear arylic top. It will have a curve to it and possibly will be an older skylight. There are two types of arylic skylights which is curb-mounted or self-flashed. Please scroll down to the arylic part of the page.

Scroll below to the arylic skylight section

Glass Skylights

Glass skylights will have glass instead of arylic and will be flat. There are three types of glass skylights. If you are unsure of what type of skylight you will need to scroll down to the glass section of this page.

Scroll below to the glass skylight section

If your skylight is arylic

Arylic Curb-Mounted Skylight

Curb-Mounted skylights can be easily identified because they are installed on top of a site-built curb constructed from dimensional lumber.They will traditionally consist of two rows of screws around the perimter of the skylight.

Arylic Self-Flashed Skylight

Self-flashed skylights are found on shingle roofs, but may be found on other roof types as well. Distinguishing factors with an acrylic self-flashed skylight is the extended retainer ring, single row of retainer screws, and the tall thorat that extends off the roof.

If your skylight is glass

Velux Self-Flashed Skylight

Self-flashed skylights differ more from both deck and curb-mounted skylights because their curb and flashing are built into the skylight.

Velux Curb-Mounted Skylight

Curb-mounted skylights are the most common installation preference in low-pitch or flat-roof situations.

Velux Deck-Mounted Skylight

Deck-mounted skylights nail or screw down to the roof decking and will typically have a low profile on the roof.
